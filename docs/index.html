Sovereign legal AI for Qatar.

Maha is a self-hosted document assistant that handles both Qatar's mainland civil law and the QFC common law system, in ten of the languages spoken in Qatar's courts and workplaces.

Forks Emilie (Swiss legal AI), which forks Mike.

Named after

Sheikha Maha bint Mansour Al Thani

The first female Qatari judge, appointed to the Court of Cassation in 2010 in courts that had until then been entirely male. She belongs in the stack.

Why this exists

Most legal AI was not built with Qatar in mind.

Two legal systems, one workspace

Mainland Qatar is a civil law jurisdiction with codified statutes in Arabic. The Qatar Financial Centre is a parallel common law jurisdiction with its own courts and English-language judgments. Most legal AI handles one or the other. Maha handles both, and disambiguates which applies before answering.

Built for everyone in the courtroom

Lawyers, paralegals, NGOs — and the migrant workers whose rights they defend. Maha responds in Arabic, English, Urdu, Hindi, Tagalog, Bengali, Nepali, Malayalam, French and Persian, while citing the authoritative Arabic or English source text.

Your data stays in Qatar

Self-hosted on infrastructure you control. Designed around Fanar (QCRI's open-weights LLM) and Qatar-resident hosts like MEEZA and Ooredoo Cloud. Public Qatar legal sources are mirrored as MCP servers running inside your perimeter.

Capabilities

What Maha does today

Bilingual document chat

Upload Arabic or English contracts. Ask questions, extract clauses, summarise, compare versions. Citations are pinned to the verbatim source text and the page it appeared on.

Dual-system aware

The system prompt teaches the model Qatar's two regimes. Ask about a QFC matter and you will not get mainland answers. Where ambiguous, Maha asks before assuming.

Tabular review

Run structured clause extraction across hundreds of documents at once. Get a comparable matrix instead of a stack of summaries.

Workflows

Define reusable AI workflows that run automatically against any document. Ship templates for common Qatari work: kafala documents, QFC contracts, employment files, family matters.

Live legal search

Four MCP servers wrap the public Qatar legal portals: Al Meezan, QFC Court (496+ judgments verified live), Qatar Official Gazette, and the Ministry of Justice. Citations come back with authoritative source URLs.

Migrant labour access

Ask in Urdu or Tagalog about your end-of-service benefits and get an answer grounded in the Qatar Labour Law and Wage Protection System, with the relevant Arabic article cited verbatim.

Roadmap

An honest plan, in public.

Q2 2026 Shipped

Foundations

  • Fork from Emilie. Rebrand. Sovereign stack documented.
  • System prompt teaches Qatar's dual jurisdiction.
  • Four MCP servers built — QFC Court verified live.
  • Locale scaffold for ten languages with RTL flags.
Q3 2026 In progress

Inside-Qatar deploy

  • First deploy on MEEZA. Verify all four MCP scrapers against live portals.
  • Fanar integration end to end — both self-hosted vLLM and the QCRI managed API.
  • UI internationalisation shipping for all ten languages, RTL polish.
  • Document chat private beta with the first design partner.
Q4 2026 Planned

Public beta

  • Public beta open to law firms and in-house teams.
  • Workflow library for common Qatari matters: kafala documents, QFC contracts, employment files, family law.
  • Voice input for migrant-rights flows where literacy is a barrier.
  • First NGO pilot for migrant worker labour access in Urdu and Tagalog.
Q1 2027 Planned

General availability

  • Native Arabic UI polish — typography, RTL nuance.
  • Court e-filing integrations where Qatari courts permit.
  • Audit logs and document retention policies fit for regulated firms.
  • Multi-tenant deployment guide for legal aid organisations.

Build with us

A short list of people we'd love to hear from.

Maha is open source under AGPL-3.0. Issues and pull requests are welcome from anyone. Some specific gaps where help would compound:

Qatari-licensed lawyers

Review the system prompt, the workflow library, and the disambiguation rules between mainland civil law and the QFC. Tell us where the model is glib and where it is wrong.

Arabic-fluent front-end engineers

Land the RTL polish for Arabic, Urdu, Persian. Typography, input direction, mirrored layouts, the small pixel decisions that make a product feel native instead of translated.

Migrant-rights organisations

Pilot the Urdu, Tagalog, Bengali, and Nepali flows with real cases. Help us build the right workflow templates for end-of-service, kafala transfers, wage protection complaints.

Sovereign infrastructure operators

MEEZA, Ooredoo Cloud, QCRI Fanar team — if you operate the stack Maha is designed for, tell us how to be a good citizen of it. We will document, contribute back, and credit.
